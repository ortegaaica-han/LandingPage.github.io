Work-in-Progress

By Aica Ortega • February 2026

Building Sandbox Level 1 Quest Final Reflection
"Gaano na ba karaming oras ang naubos natin sa pag-grind? If you’re like me, gaming isn’t just a hobby; it’s a lifestyle."

Building My Dream Sandbox

Let’s be real—from the high-stakes strategy of outsmarting opponents to the therapeutic routine of harvesting crops, I’ve done it all. But lately, habang naglalaro ako, hindi ko maiwasang mapaisip: “Paano kung ganito ang mechanics nito?” That’s when it hit me. I don't just want to play; I want to create.

Hindi lang ako basta player—I want to be the one behind the screen creating these worlds. My dream is to build my own game once I finally get that degree, and honestly? I’m already playing it in my head.

In my ideal world, bakit kailangan nating mamili? I love open-world games because of the freedom, but I also crave the discipline of strategy and the chill vibes of farming. My dream game is a mix of all these. Imagine a world where you don’t just fight monsters for the sake of it, but you actually have to manage a settlement, farm resources to survive, and use actual strategy to defend your land. Hindi lang siya puro pindot; kailangan ng utak at pasensya. I want to create a loop where every action—whether it’s planting a seed or wielding a sword—actually matters to the story.

The Level 1 Quest

I'll be honest, sometimes nakaka-inip mag-aral ng theory when you just want to start coding, but you can’t break the rules of game design until you know them. Getting my degree is my "Level 1" quest. It’s where I’ll learn the technical backbone—the math and logic—needed so my game doesn't crash in the first ten minutes. I’m treating college as my character’s training arc. Every project and exam is just a skill point I’m adding to my build.

Building a game is a marathon. Alam kong hindi madali, and there will be bugs and sleepless nights ahead. But every time I hold a controller now, I see a challenge. My future game won't just be mechanics; it will be a reflection of my journey.

My Takeaway: Don't just consume the things you love—analyze them. My future game won't just be a collection of mechanics; it will be a reflection of everything I’ve learned as a gamer and a student. Soon, I won't just be playing someone else's world; I'll be inviting you into mine.
