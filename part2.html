Grind, Pull, Repeat

By Aica Ortega • February 2026

The Ritual The Rules The Escape

The Weekend Ritual

Alam mo yung feeling na pagod na pagod ka na sa buong linggo? Between the endless deadlines sa work and the heavy load sa school, minsan feeling ko sasabog na yung utak ko. But the moment Friday night hits, I have this one "habit" that instantly resets my system. No, hindi ako lumalabas para uminom. Instead, I open my favorite game, look at my saved-up currency, and prepare for the "pull."

I call it my healthy gambling addiction. It sounds like a contradiction, 'di ba? But let me explain how this "addiction" actually keeps me sane.

The Rules of the Grind

The first rule of my "gambling" is simple: Walang totoong pera. I am a proud member of the F2P (Free-to-Play) community. My "gambling" isn't fueled by my bank account or my allowance; it’s fueled by my effort.

Every day, kahit pagod ako, I spend a few minutes doing my daily quests and grinding for materials. Para sa akin, may halaga yung bawat in-game gem or ticket kasi pinaghirapan ko siya. There’s a certain kind of discipline involved in saving up for months just to wait for that one character or item banner. It’s not about the money; it’s about the payoff of the grind.

Finding the Escape

When the weekend arrives, doon na pumapasok yung "escape" part. This is the time when I finally get to use everything I’ve saved.

Kapag nag-o-open na ako ng loot boxes or nag-wi-wish sa gacha banners, nawawala yung stress ko about school or work. In those moments, hindi ko iniisip yung reports na kailangang ipasa o yung exams sa Monday. My world shrinks down to just the screen, the music, and the hope of seeing that "Gold Glow."

Even if I don’t get what I want, the process of playing and strategizing how to get more resources is enough to give my brain a much-needed break from reality.

Sa totoo lang, it’s not really about the virtual items. It’s about having a "switch" that I can turn on to signal my brain that work-time is over and play-time has begun. I’ve realized that this hobby has taught me a lot about patience and managing expectations. If I win the RNG (Random Number Generation) today, great! If not, okay lang—mag-iipon ulit ako. It’s a low-stakes way to feel excitement without the real-world consequences of losing money.

"To anyone feeling burnt out: find your own 'grind.' Whether it’s gaming, collecting, or any hobby that lets you switch off your 'work brain,' embrace it."

For me, as long as I’m not touching my saved allowance money and I’m still having fun, I’ll keep on rolling. It’s my way of winning back my peace of mind.
